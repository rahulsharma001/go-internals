## Current framework (calibrated)

### Principles locked in

1. **80/20 spine** — Interview signal concentrates in concurrency, memory/GC, interfaces, errors, and core value semantics. [[Study Plan]] names the **exact topic packs** (Waves A–G) that map to those bands.
2. **Daily Revision mirrors the spine** — Each week has a **Focus band** (full blurt) and **Maintenance band** (5-second / skim). Most morning minutes go to Focus, not equal coverage.
3. **Retrieval > passive reading** — Predict-the-output, trick questions, verbal gold answers, mocks — **not** high-volume MCQs as the primary mode ([[Study Plan]] §10).
4. **Applications parallel fluency** — Gates in [[Study Plan]] §7 separate **practice employers** from **target/dream** loops; earlier apps are calibration + logging, not proof of completion.

## Self-audit checklist (quality bar for this framework)

Use before trusting any week's plan:

- [ ] **Pack honesty:** Does this week's Focus band contain only topics from the active wave in [[Study Plan]] §2?
- [ ] **Dependency honesty:** Am I skipping a prerequisite (see [[Roadmap]]) to chase speed?
- [ ] **Gate honesty:** Am I applying at dream employers before Gate 1 evidence without accepting that's calibration risk?
- [ ] **Revision honesty:** Is Maintenance truly lighter than Focus (time + depth)?
- [ ] **Mock honesty:** At least **two** mock reps scheduled this week (technical and/or behavioral)?

If any box fails, adjust the week before scaling applications.

## Research notes (reference only)

Aggregated public discussions (Reddit, forums, guides) consistently emphasize:

- **Concurrency + interfaces + production reasoning** dominate senior Go loops.
- **Pure volume** (hundreds of disconnected facts) underperforms **trade-off explanations** + **timed verbal answers** + **mocks**.
- **Timeline:** A **first pass** through a high-ROI pack can be weeks; **fluent performance** under interview pressure typically needs additional retrieval + mocks — [[Study Plan]] §3–§4 states this explicitly.

Company lists and compensation figures drift quickly; treat employer-specific detail as **research you refresh at apply time**, not something embedded in static markdown here.
